Date 6/6/68

On June 5, 1963, SA FREDRICK &, BECKER, FBI, observed
the search of 6 1956 De Soto, two-door, V-8, pink and white in
color, bearing 1963 California license JWS 093, and Vehicle
Identification No. 620590511, which was registered to SIRHAN B.

_ SIRHAN, 696 Bast Howard Street, Pasadena, California. This
examination was conducted by Officer JAMES #, CLABORN, Badge
6023, Special Investigation Division (SID), Los Angeles Police
Department (LAPD), under the authority of a local search warrant.

Examination of the vehicle revealed the presence of a
"Tos Angeles Times" newspaper issued June 5, 1968. The odometer
reading of the vehicle was found to be $0963.3. What appeared
to be two spent .22 caliber lead projectiles were found on top
of the front seat on the passenger side of the automobile. One
business card from the "Lock Stock and Barrel" gun and fishing
equipment store, 3972 Bast Huntington Drive, San Gabriel,
California, was found on the rear floor of the automobile on
the passenger side. Officer CLABORN examined the contents of
the glove compartment and found one empty box of CCI Mini Mag
High Power .22 caliber bullets. One sales receipt, No. 2372,
of the Lock Stock and Barrel store was found in the glove
compartment and this receipt reflected the purchase of four boxes
of .22 caliber bullets for the sum of $2.99 which was paid
for in cash on June 1, 1968. A wallet holding identification
in the name of SIRHAN SIRHAN was located in the glove compartment.
The trunk of the automobile was found to contain three old spare
tires and numerous rags. A book entitled "Healing-The Divine
Art" was found in the baci: seat of the automobile.

Officer CLABORN advised that the automobile was
to-be impounded by the LAPD at the time of the examination.
He further advised that official Lists of items taken from
the car could be secured through him and Officer C. D. WHITE,
Officer CLABORN processed the automobile for latent fingerprints
and advised that he obtained several fairly good impressions
from the steering wheel area and the glove compartment.

Lt. CHARLES HUGHES, Rampart Division, Los Angeles
Police Department, Los Angeles, California, advised that he
testified before the Los Angeles County Grand Jury on June 7,
1968. HUGHES related that he assisted Officers A. PLACENTIA
and T. WHITE to make out an evidence report in connection
with the arrest of SIRHAN B. SIRHAN while SIRHAN was in the
interrogation room. He then gave PLACENTIA and WHITE a key
found in the possession of SIRHAN-and requested them to search
for SIRHAN's car. HUGHES stated the key was returned to him
at which time he gave the key to Lt. A. S. HEGGE, attached to
the Rampart Division, who obtained a search warrant to search
a vehicle believed to belong to STRHAN. HUGHES advised the
key was returned to him by Lt. HEGGE and he entered the key into
evidence at the Grand Jury hearing.
